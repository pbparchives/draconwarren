As you near, you can see that several different items are for sale: Rat Chunks in Sewer Sauce; timbermen's cakes w. sap; and some sort of deep-fried potato cakes.

Her customers are as varied as orc businessmen with their tradesman's bags in hand, occasionally checking the water clock in the center of town, just a few dozen yards away; Schwartzalven mothers trying to retain control of their inky broods; and the occasional pick-pocket 'earning' his or her meal in line.

There is no sign of any other adventerous/'loyal' souls at the Temple, and the scaffolds which are dangling next to the now-draped mural are unattended -- the workers sitting on the cobblestones eating their meals, and drinking their steaming chickory-brew.

The Styxtrene Temple, a converted temple of a long-forgotten demi-god, is as run down as everything else in this quarter.

The acolyte gestures for you to follow him in to the temple.

Your keen dwarven nose detects more than a hint of gold within the dimly lit interior, incense overflowing as he opens the left pannel of a double door, which still shows sign of old battering -- apparently from smaller axe blades.

"I had hoped that your group would be larger...", the man hesitates looking at you with a faint, nervous smile.

"Follow me, please, sir.", He says as he begins to dissaper into the darkness.

As you follow, you begin to understand the layout of the temple's construction, due to your inherent dwarven abilities -- and, perhaps more importantly, sense that you are nearing a large concentration of worked gold.

"Daergesh, have we any other takers?", a smoothe, woman's voice asks from the room. Daergesh -- the acolyte -- is leading you to.

"Yes, my lady. A stout dwarf, and capable, it appears--", Daergesh begins, but is cut short.

"Your opinion is hardly based on experience. I won't be needing it. You are dismissed to your pressing duties, Daergesh.", the same voice, now slightly harsher.

You are shown in, by the now subserviant and shamed Daergesh, who immediately leaves, further down the dark, perfumed hallway.

The room you are at the threshold of is a smallish affair, perhaps ten by eight, with a ten foot ceiling. It is covered in black laqured wood, intricately carven with human figures and lurking creatures which almost seem to ooze along as light plays across its surface.

There is a massive desk, which takes up most of the floor space. It too is made of black laqured wood, but of a more spectacular design -- including wildly raised projections from the legs, and decorated with images of the Death Empress on her various victories both on the surface world, as well as in nightmarish underworld scenes.

Your mouth nearly agape at the craftsmanship, you slowly raise your eys to peer over the top, to the woman sitting behind the desk.

She is a purple-skinned woman, somewhat elven looking, though clearly a halfbreen of some sort -- likely schwarzalven and human. She is possessed of such similar features to the Empress herself that you feel uneasy in her presnce.

"Ah, a dawrven magician, I see...", she says, smiling wickedly.

"Would you care for some wine this morning?", she offers, standing slightly from her seated position, leaning over the immaculate desk's surface, her ample boosom and spidry waist accented by a black lace and leather dress, decorated with dangling silver orniments of agonized-looking skulls of many types of creatures -- some of which you can not readily identify.

"Lady Silkervish re'Hakt, Priestess in Her service." Again, her wicked smile, flashing snow-white teeth, sends a chill down your spine. "Please, have a seat."

A slight creak to your left causes you to turn, and you see a form writhing in the wall panneling begin to separate itself...
