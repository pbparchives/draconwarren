"This door is too small for me,’ said Quogh.

"Me too," said Vin. "Let’s go on."

They walked on. The further they got from the entrance with its two glowing stones, the dimmer the light became. The tunneld tilted to the right at a 45 degree angle for about 30 feet then straightened out again. They then noticed a door on the left hand side of the wall.

It was a standard dungeon door, ten feet wide, ten feet high. It had a latch on the handle and the door obviously opened outward. The three adventurers roamed over to examine it.

"No magic on this door," said Dekh.

"No sign either," said Vin. He put his hand on the latch. It gave slightly. "Doesn’t seem to be locked either."

Quogh put his head up against the stone and listened. "I hear some noises, but I can’t really tell what’s making them," he said.

Vin put his face up against the crack of the door. "I smell something," he said, "but I can’t tell what it is. Something alive, I think."

"Shall we open it and see what’s inside?" said Dekh.
